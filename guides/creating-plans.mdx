Study plans are the foundation of your learning journey on Study Sync. This guide walks you through creating a comprehensive study plan from scratch.

## What is a Study Plan?

A study plan is a curated collection of learning resources (YouTube videos, PDFs, articles, and more) organized for a specific course or topic. Once created, you can start an instance to track your progress through the materials.

## Creating Your First Study Plan

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Create Plan">
    Click the **Create Plan** button from your dashboard or the "My Plans" page. This will take you to the study plan creation form.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill in Basic Information">
    Provide the essential details about your study plan:
    
    - **Title**: A clear, descriptive name for your plan (e.g., "Introduction to Machine Learning")
    - **Course Code**: The course identifier (e.g., "CSE101", "MATH202")
    - **Short Description**: A brief one-line summary of what the plan covers
    - **Full Description**: Optional detailed information about the learning objectives and content
    - **Public/Private**: Toggle whether other users can discover and use your plan
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Resources">
    Build your study plan by adding learning materials. Study Sync supports multiple resource types:
    
    **YouTube Videos**
    - Paste any YouTube video URL
    - The system automatically fetches the title and duration
    
    **YouTube Playlists**
    - Add an entire playlist at once
    - All videos are added individually for granular progress tracking
    
    **PDF Documents**
    - Provide the PDF URL
    - Enter the title and number of pages
    - Set estimated minutes per page (default: 3 minutes)
    
    **Articles & Blog Posts**
    - Paste the article URL
    - Enter the title and estimated reading time in minutes
    
    **Google Drive Links**
    - Share files stored in Google Drive
    - Optionally add a custom title and time estimate
    
    **Custom Links**
    - Add any other web resource
    - Must provide a title
    - Optionally add time estimate

    Click **Add Resource** after filling in the details for each item.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Organize Resources">
    After adding resources, you can:
    
    - **Reorder**: Drag and drop resources to arrange them in the optimal learning sequence
    - **Remove**: Click the trash icon to delete resources you no longer need
    - **Preview**: See the total time and resource count update in real-time
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save Your Plan">
    Once you're satisfied with your study plan:
    
    - Review all information for accuracy
    - Click **Create Study Plan**
    - You'll be redirected to the plan details page
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Best Practices

<Tip>
  **Start Small**: Begin with 5-10 resources for your first plan. You can always edit and add more later.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Logical Order**: Arrange resources from foundational concepts to advanced topics. Think about how someone new to the subject would learn best.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Accurate Time Estimates**: Provide realistic time estimates for PDFs and articles. This helps with scheduling and progress tracking.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Descriptive Titles**: Use clear, specific titles that indicate what each resource covers, especially for custom links.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Resources are shared across all plans. If you add a YouTube video that already exists in the system, Study Sync will use the existing resource instead of creating a duplicate.
</Warning>

## Managing Visibility

When creating a plan, you can control who can access it:

- **Private Plans**: Only you and people you explicitly share with can see and start instances
- **Public Plans**: Anyone can discover, view, and start instances of your plan

You can change this setting anytime by editing your plan.

## What's Next?

After creating your study plan:

1. **Share with collaborators** if you want others to help edit the plan
2. **Start an instance** to begin tracking your progress through the materials
3. **Edit anytime** to add new resources or update information

See [Sharing & Collaborating](/guides/sharing-collaborating) to learn how to work with others on your plans.
